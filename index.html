Methodology Mechanics

The Imagination Activation Cycle

Instead of starting with complex interfaces or dry syntax, the framework initiates a four-stage psychological loop that connects tools directly to human aspiration.

Hook the Learner's Imagination First

"We begin with what they desire to create, never with what the AI tool does."

Traditional training walks in and explains UI layouts. The Chrissie Method hooks imagination emotionally by asking learners to map out their core dream first. Once the dream is vivid, AI is introduced as the key to unlocking it.

Psychological Goal

Reactivate child-like imagination, override tech intimidation, and secure deep personal stake.

Tactical Hook

Start with a core aspiration or deep personal memory rather than dry code syntax.

WSQ Curriculum Example

Dream Holiday Project: Instead of "Here is Designs.ai", we ask: *“Imagine your dream holiday. Where would you go? How would it feel?”* Then, we paint the vision using AI co-pilots.

Low-Stakes, High-Play Discovery

"Play, tinker, fail, and iterate in a safe sandbox environment."

Instead of giving strict click-by-click instructions, we give learners freedom to command the AI. We design tasks where failure is impossible and experimentation is rewarded. Each self-discovery generates massive dopamine wins.

Psychological Goal

Overcome fear of breaking the system, build early physical muscle memory, and cultivate self-efficacy.

Tactical Hook

Prompt-guided exploration with prompts that shift in difficulty: Simple Template ➔ Edits ➔ Scratch.

Prompting Essentials Example

Explore Stage: *“What happens if you change this adjective? What visual style did the AI return? Did it shock or satisfy you?”* The learner discovers the mechanic on their own.

Self-Articulation Breeds Ownership

"When learners verbalize the lesson, they anchor it in their own identity."

If a trainer summarizes the lesson, the learner remains a passive receiver. In our debrief, we place the spotlight back on the learner. By answering deep reflective questions, they conceptualize the framework in their own terms.

Psychological Goal

Lock in tactical insights, build critical evaluation skills, and transition knowledge to long-term memory.

Tactical Hook

Self-reflective verbalization prompts: *“What surprised you? What did you override? Why did it happen?”*

Debrief Example

Seniors in Chrissie's courses share: *“I realized the AI is just expanding my raw feelings. I am the writer; it is simply my high-speed translator.”*

Real-World Autonomous Missions

"They leave with a concrete personal mission, not just static notes."

A transformation is only complete when it leaves the classroom. The final step is an autonomous application task. Every learner constructs a real-life deliverable that holds actual professional value or deeply personal meaning to share with family.

Psychological Goal

Build permanent agency, cement pride, and establish immediate real-world ROI.

Tactical Hook

Assign a highly personal, shareable asset (e.g. storybooks, greeting cards, team prompts).

Apply Example

Real-World Task: *“Create one enhanced memory design this week and share it directly with a loved one.”* They become proud advocates of AI potential.

We immediately establish safety. No programming environments are shown. We start by listening to their personal stories, activating dreams, and showing them that their human judgment is the most valuable asset.

Educational DNA Code

System Architecture 
You are helping design and deliver an AI education experience that:

1. ACTIVATES IMAGINATION, not just transfers information
   - Start with learner's dream/goal, not the tool
   - Use AI to make their vision real
   - Design projects around what THEY want to create

2. KEEPS LEARNER IN DIRECTOR'S SEAT
   - AI is co-pilot, learner decides
   - Emphasize human judgment stays central
   - Help learners understand when/how to override AI
   - Build confidence that they're in control

3. TRANSFORMS CONFIDENCE + AGENCY
   - Design small wins first, build momentum
   - Help learner articulate their own learning
   - Celebrate THEIR choices, not the tool's output
   - Show them evidence they're transforming

When you help me design a course/exercise/prompt:
- Lead with imagination activation (what do they want to create?)
- Design for their discovery (not my telling)
- Build confidence scaffolding (small → medium → challenging)
- Include psychology elements (celebration, identity shift, pride)
- Ensure ethics is integrated
- Make sure they leave feeling unstoppable, not replaced
